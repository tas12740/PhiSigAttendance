Taylor

Dear Phi Sig:

I’ve sat down several times to do this now, and each time the words that I have in my head just don’t come out onto the page. But none of that came out quite right, so this time I’ll just speak from the heart.

Phi Sig, to me, has always been about the people. It is the people I met on a trip to see the solar eclipse (which, wildly enough to me, happened while many of you were still in high school) that convinced me to jump out of my comfort zone and go to an interest meeting. (You could also argue I was just following Adam because I had no ability to go out and do my own thing and, while that’s savage, it’s probably true, though I digress.) It was Cat, who was the first stranger I ever met to make me friend them on Snapchat, and Matt, who everyone kept making jokes about, and Wade, this strange old man who for some reason was tagging along with a bunch of college kids, who were Adam’s fam but would become my fam. These people would give me an introduction to the group of people who have change my life. The people I have met in Phi Sig have expanded my perspective, helped me to think about the world in different ways, made me more intentional, and taught me more about life and love than they could ever know. It is these people that have formed the foundation of my college experience, and with whom I’ve made so many incredible memories. So, to the Best Damn Fam Phi Sig has to offer (Matt, Micah, Wade, Cat, Michael, Brooks, Maris, Adam, Sarah V., Maggie, Evan, Michelle, Sarah S., Tori, Anna P., Abby, Grace, Vignesh, Anna H., and, of course, my two wonderful littles, Katie and Emily) and the many, many others (among whom Libby and the greatest class ever, the Beta Zetas, deserve special shoutouts) in Phi Sig who have touched my soul and made these years to remember: thank you for making me the person I am today. You gave me a community and a place on campus, and I will forever be grateful for that.

Phi Sig at its heart is caught in a crisis of never knowing what exactly it is. What attracted me to it was that it seemed like this place that offered a route into so many different things, whether it be doing service or learning new things, all while offering a close-knit social community. It’s not merely a one-dimensional place. At the same time, I always tell PNMs that Phi Sig can be what you put into it. It has become my primary “thing” on campus, and I have chosen to dedicate my free time and energy to it, but it does not have to be that for everyone. It has given me things to put on my resume, allowed me to give back to my community, and taught me such varied things as salsa dancing and painting. It has, as I’ve said, given me friends and a community to last a lifetime. However, what Phi Sig sometimes fails to do is to distinguish between these various missions. Is it a primarily social organization which strikes out into the realms of service and scholarship? Is it an organization with higher goals that also allows its siblings to form a community and connections? I believe that it can be all of the above: a place for people to find a community of different people which allows them to grow by giving them opportunities to learn new things, serve their local community, and become leaders, all while meeting fantastic new people. It can be a place for people like me, who needed a place on campus, to be their thing and allow them to give back to it in turn, but it can also be an auxiliary piece to round out students and their college experiences. It is, however, no longer my place, and it’s up to you all to shape Phi Sig into the place you want it to be.

I know that Phi Sig is in great hands for the future (and I hope it would, given that I’ve helped choose all of you). I am incredibly excited to see what the future holds for all of you as people and for Phi Sig as an organization. Continue to be the smart, motivated, layered, incredible souls I know all of you are. You provided me a light in my darkness, and I know that you will continue to do so for so many others. Make the most of every day. Take moments to breathe, and remember that you are all capable of greatness. Stay up all night talking with your friends sometimes, even if there's a lot going on. Hold your friends and family close and always know that you are worthy.

It has been the honor of my life to serve you as your IT chair, IM/Rec chair, and, finally, Parliamentarian. I hope that I have done right by you all and Phi Sig and, along the way, that I perhaps put a smile on some of your faces or made some of you laugh. I am the person I am today because I met all of you. I will continue to be in the area next year, so please don’t be strangers. If you ever want to reach out to an old man, you can text me at (919)-622-5071 for any reason. Please take care of my baby.

There’s a great quote I found recently:

“Do not be dismayed by the brokenness of the world. All things break, and all things can be mended, not with time, as they say, but with intention. So go. Love intentionally, extravagantly, unconditionally. The broken world waits in darkness for the light that is you.”

Though this may be the end of my journey at UNC, I will carry you all in my heart forever and I hope that this will not conclude our journeys together. I love you all, and as I like to say, my hope that this is not a goodbye, but a see you soon.

Taylor
